Project Information
Literature Review

Many studies have explored the potential of blockchain technology to enhance the security of Electronic Health Records (EHR), especially in resource-constrained healthcare environments like Sri Lanka's clinics. Blockchain offers a secure and adaptable foundation for safeguarding EHR systems, addressing security vulnerabilities in healthcare systems. Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) have shown promise in information security, allowing the demonstration of data possession without revealing the actual data, offering future-proof, tamper-proof, and resilient security against various threats. Additionally, Homomorphic Encryption (HE) enables data computations on encrypted data, preserving patient privacy. These technologies have been applied in EHR systems and other healthcare applications, catering to both large and smaller healthcare institutions. Moreover, innovative approaches, such as data splitting, encryption, and attribute-based authentication using blockchain platforms, enhance data sharing while ensuring privacy and security, offering valuable insights and solutions to various challenges in the healthcare sector

Research Gap

In Sri Lankan clinics, there are few to no EHR systems in place. Most clinics have their own systems that we cannot access or monitor due to privacy concerns. However, no health record system contains privacy-protection or encryption features. Our proposed system aims to provide a secure solution for maintaining patients' electronic health records (EHR). There are no effective methods for addressing secure access to PHR while maintaining privacy in cloud systems. Since there is a slow adaptation rate on EHR systems with present technology, proposed system was implemented on novel technologies and methodologies.

Research Problem

Small healthcare clinics struggle to maintain patient health records, leading to privacy and accountability issues. The proposed solution is a secure cloud storage system that simplifies record management, enhances privacy, and modernizes healthcare administration.

Identified research problems:

  • Lack of PHR privacy when shared with external entities (patient has less control over shared PHI)
  • Patients have no control or knowledge over who, when, and the level of information accessed, and there is no way to prevent someone from accessing the data outside the treatment process.
  • Password compromise, phishing, and password stealing can impact on privacy.
Research Objectives

Main Objective

The main objective is to propose an EHR system that utilizes modern technological advancements to secure data while providing the accessibility of the system to healthcare providers that are smaller in scale. Increasing the visibility of medical data of smaller scale establishments and strengthening overall healthcare infrastructure via an updated, accessible, and secured approach.

Specific Objectives
  • Implement web-based ZKP authentication to provide a robust authentication process that can prevent unauthorized access to the system.
  • Synchronize the off-chain database in real-time with the system cloud backup.
  • Initiating Consent management and Secure data sharing along with Granular Time-Scoped Role-Based Access Control (GTRBAC).
  • Secure data shared with external entities along with need-to-know basis.
  • A user-friendly claiming process that Validates and sanitizes user-inputted data with an extended notification system.
  • Securing disclosed PHI (by consent of the patient) use, by external entities using privacy-preserving information retrieval (PIR) and processing technologies.
  • Managing secure backups of patient data integrating On-premises and cloud systems.
  • Maintaining logs related to cloud component usage for non-repudiation.
